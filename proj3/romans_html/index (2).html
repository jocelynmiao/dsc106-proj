The Uneven Heat: where the planet warms fastest under each emissions future.

Climate change is usually reported as a single global temperature, but that number masks profoundly uneven regional warming. This explorer lets you compare how four climate zones — tropical, subtropical, temperate, and polar — respond under three emissions pathways, from aggressive policy action (SSP1-2.6) to fossil-fueled growth (SSP5-8.5).

04 / Data

Data & transformations

↳ write here

Describe the data source and every transformation you applied. The rubric specifically rewards clarity here.

  • Source: CMIP6 / CESM2-WACCM, accessed via Pangeo on Google Cloud
  • Experiments used: historical (1850–2014) + SSP1-2.6 + SSP2-4.5 + SSP5-8.5 (2015–2100)
  • Regional aggregation: 4 climate zones by absolute latitude (tropical 0–23.5°, subtropical 23.5–40°, temperate 40–60°, polar 60–90°)
  • Annual mean from monthly data
  • Baseline: 1951–1980 (NASA GISTEMP standard)
  • Anomaly = annual mean − baseline (per region)
  • Global mean = area-weighted average of regional anomalies (tropical 39.8%, subtropical 24.5%, temperate 22.3%, polar 13.4%)
  • Amplification = regional anomaly − global mean
  • Important framing: these are simulation outputs, not predictions; we are not training a model
