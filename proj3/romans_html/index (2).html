The Uneven Heat: where the planet warms fastest under each emissions future.

Climate change is usually reported as a single global temperature, but that number masks profoundly uneven regional warming. This explorer lets you compare how four climate zones — tropical, subtropical, temperate, and polar — respond under three emissions pathways, from aggressive policy action (SSP1-2.6) to fossil-fueled growth (SSP5-8.5).

View 1 · All regions, one scenario

How regions diverge under each emissions pathway.

Each line tracks one climate zone's annual mean temperature anomaly relative to the 1951–1980 baseline. The dashed gray line is the global mean — the standard "climate change" headline number. Watch how regional lines spread apart from it as you scrub through scenarios.
